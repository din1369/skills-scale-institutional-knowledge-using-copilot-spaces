# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Core Roles

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Extended & Supporting Roles

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove blockers, and coach the team in agile practices. They work closely with Project Managers to ensure the team can execute efficiently and maintain sustainable pace.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and help remove blockers that impede team progress
- Coach team members on agile practices and continuous improvement
- Protect the team from scope creep and external interruptions
- Track sprint metrics (velocity, burndown) and facilitate data-driven planning

### Goals
- Enable the team to deliver consistent, predictable value
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement
- Remove impediments to team productivity

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- One-on-ones with team members to address blockers
- Escalation to PM for external blockers or resource constraints
- Retro action items and follow-up tracking

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads own the quality strategy, test execution, and acceptance criteria validation. They collaborate with Developers and Product Managers to ensure deliverables meet requirements and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Define test plans and quality acceptance criteria for features
- Design and execute manual and automated test scenarios
- Track and triage defects, ensuring critical issues are escalated
- Collaborate with Developers on testability and test automation
- Ensure acceptance criteria are clear and verifiable

### Goals
- Deliver high-quality, defect-free software to production
- Reduce time spent on manual testing through automation
- Maintain customer trust through reliable products

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and acceptance criteria refinement
- Test results and defect reports in pull request reviews
- Weekly quality metrics and burn-down status
- Post-release smoke tests and incident triage

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers drive alignment on user interface standards, user experience, and design consistency. They partner with Product Managers and Developers to ensure features are intuitive and meet user needs.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing to inform design decisions
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specs for new features
- Establish and maintain design systems and component libraries
- Collaborate with Developers to ensure designs are implemented accurately
- Iterate on designs based on user feedback and metrics

### Goals
- Create intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Ensure consistency across all user-facing products
- Reduce support burden through better design

### Typical Communication
- Design review sessions during sprint planning
- Design specs and component documentation in repositories
- Usability testing findings and recommendations
- Feedback loops with Developers during implementation

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers manage CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure, and deployment automation. They enable fast, reliable releases and support observability across production systems.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines for automated testing and deployment
- Manage cloud infrastructure, networking, and security configurations
- Implement monitoring, logging, and alerting for production systems
- Support incident response and post-mortems with infrastructure insights
- Automate operational tasks to reduce manual toil and human error

### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable, and repeatable deployments
- Minimize downtime and incident impact through observability
- Reduce operational burden through automation

### Typical Communication
- Release coordination during deployment windows
- Infrastructure and deployment documentation
- On-call rotations and incident response
- Post-incident reviews and automation improvements

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business stakeholders and delivery teams by capturing requirements, analyzing processes, and ensuring alignment between business goals and technical solutions.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements and user stories
- Analyze current processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Create process diagrams, user journey maps, and acceptance criteria
- Validate requirements with stakeholders before development begins
- Support trade-off analysis and prioritization with business impact data

### Goals
- Ensure delivered solutions address business needs accurately
- Reduce rework by clarifying requirements upfront
- Maximize return on investment for development efforts

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- User story and acceptance criteria documentation
- Process diagrams and business impact analysis
- Weekly alignment with PM/PdM on scope and priority

---

## Executive Sponsor / Key Stakeholder

### Role Summary
Executive Sponsors provide vision, strategic direction, and resources for projects. They resolve escalated blockers, ensure alignment with organizational strategy, and act as advocates for the project.

### Responsibilities
- Define strategic vision and business objectives for projects
- Allocate budget, headcount, and organizational resources
- Resolve escalated blockers and business-impacting decisions
- Provide executive-level updates to leadership and boards
- Ensure project alignment with organizational priorities and values

### Goals
- Deliver strategic initiatives aligned with organizational goals
- Maximize business value and return on investment
- Maintain stakeholder confidence and executive alignment

### Typical Communication
- Monthly or quarterly executive reviews and steering committees
- Escalation paths for critical business decisions
- Budget and resource allocation discussions
- Public announcements and organizational communications
